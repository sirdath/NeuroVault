# NeuroVault commercial strategy

Research checked 21 July 2026. This is a product decision brief, not legal,
tax, or financial advice.

## Decision

Proceed with a paid Mac product, but treat the first release as a demand test,
not a forecasted business.

A EUR 25.99 one-time price is credible for a polished Mac productivity app.
It is not credible for a generic local notes, MCP, search, and graph bundle:
free and inexpensive products already cover those features. NeuroVault earns
the price only if the first session demonstrates the complete combination:

- relevant context arrives before the user has to request it;
- memory remains local, inspectable, portable, and provider-neutral;
- outcomes are recorded, not just prompts and notes;
- consolidation cites evidence;
- users can review and correct proposed knowledge before automatic writes;
- the consumer app makes this understandable without a terminal.

Recommended positioning:

> Automatic, local, inspectable memory that follows you across AI tools
> instead of being trapped in one provider.

Do not position MCP, embeddings, Markdown, review, or the graph alone as the
moat. Do not claim NeuroVault was the first product in this category.

## Market reality

The category is active and commercially interesting, but quickly
commoditising.

| Product | Current offer | Competitive implication |
| --- | --- | --- |

| [Bruin Notes](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/bruin-notes/id6759669768?mt=12) | Free | Local storage, on-device embeddings, graph, 60+ MCP tools, and a draft/review/publish workflow. Generic "MCP + graph + review" is not enough to charge a premium. |
| [IdeAI](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/ideai/id6754496063) | Free with one-time and subscription in-app purchases | On-device memory, portable data, MCP access, transcript imports, and an animated semantic graph already compete for the same privacy/visualisation story; its very small rating count also shows that availability is not proof of distribution. |
| [Memophant](https://memophant.co/) | USD 49.99 one-time after a 30-day trial | Demonstrates a premium direct-sale market for native, repository-resident AI memory, audits, and consolidation. Public sales figures are unavailable. |
| [ModelCaddy](https://modelcaddy.com/) | USD 29 early access, USD 49 planned one-time launch price | Very close positioning: on-device cross-tool capture, source-backed decisions, context packs, MCP setup, and a portable local vault. | [Engram for macOS](https://github.com/albertofettucini/Engram) | Free and MIT | A native Mac app with Markdown-canonical storage, local semantic recall, transcript capture, and shared MCP memory. First-party memory in ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini makes "AI memory" less novel.
It simultaneously strengthens NeuroVault's most defensible angle: one local,
auditable memory that is not owned by a single model provider.

## Name and brand risk

Run a professional trademark and App Store name clearance before spending on
launch marketing.

`NeuroVault` has long-standing prior use by [NeuroVault.org](https://neurovault.org/),
a neuroscience data repository launched years before this product. More
importantly, an unrelated [Python package named `neurovault`](https://pypi.org/project/neurovault/)
now describes itself as an AI cognitive memory layer with ingestion,
consolidation, graph search, and proactive recall. This does not establish that
either party owns an enforceable trademark, but it creates material search,
confusion, package-name, and clearance risk in the same product category.
The App Store also already contains a near-identical `NeuraVault` spelling for
an unrelated AI product. That is not the same mark or category, but it adds
search and support-confusion risk.

Do not rename impulsively. Do not submit a permanent App Store record, buy
campaign assets, or print the name into legal agreements until a qualified
search has assessed the risk.

## App Store economics

[Apple Developer Program membership](https://developer.apple.com/programs/whats-included/)
is USD 99 per year. Apple's standard commission is 30%. Eligible developers
accepted into the [App Store Small Business Program](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/small-business-program/)
receive a 15% rate while they remain within the programme's USD 1 million
proceeds threshold.

Apple defines proceeds as customer price less applicable taxes and its
commission. See [Payments and Proceeds](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/getting-paid/view-payments-and-proceeds).

Illustration for a EUR 25.99 customer price in a 20% VAT storefront:

- price excluding VAT: approximately EUR 21.66;
- proceeds at a 15% commission: approximately EUR 18.41 per sale;
- proceeds at a 30% commission: approximately EUR 15.16 per sale.

At the 15% rate, before income/corporation tax, refunds, support, and ongoing
development:

| Annual purchases | Approximate proceeds |
| ---: | ---: |
| 100 | EUR 1,841 |
| 500 | EUR 9,205 |
| 1,000 | EUR 18,410 |
| 2,000 | EUR 36,819 |
| 5,000 | EUR 92,048 |
| 10,000 | EUR 184,096 |

These are arithmetic scenarios, not sales forecasts. With a notional 20%
support and operating reserve, contribution is about EUR 14.73 per sale.

- 100 purchases validate that strangers will pay.
- 500 purchases are credible side income.
- 1,000 to 2,000 purchases can fund meaningful continued development.
- 5,000 purchases could support a small indie business.
- 10,000 purchases would require excellent distribution and should not be a
  planning baseline.

Apple reports more than 850 million average weekly App Store users across all
Apple platforms, not Mac alone. Its FY2025 statements report USD 33.708 billion
in Mac hardware revenue. Neither number provides a reliable Mac App Store unit
forecast. Paid Mac charts prove willingness to buy utilities, but Apple does
not publish the sales behind a chart rank. See [Apple's ecosystem report](https://www.apple.com/uk/newsroom/2026/06/app-store-ecosystem-reaches-1-point-4-trillion-usd-as-developers-thrive-globally/),
[FY2025 statements](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/pdfs/fy2025-q4/FY25_Q4_Consolidated_Financial_Statements.pdf),
and the [current paid Mac chart](https://apps.apple.com/us/mac/charts/36?chart=top-paid).

The financial conclusion is therefore: the channel has potential, but the
only honest revenue forecast before launch is zero. The closest new Store
competitors do not yet have enough ratings for Apple to publish a rating
overview, which is consistent with an early niche rather than proof of a large
market. App Store presence is a checkout and trust channel, not a substitute
for distribution.

## Pricing model

One-time purchase fits a local product with little ongoing cloud cost. EUR
25.99 paid upfront is the simplest launch if the listing and onboarding can
show the memory loop convincingly before purchase.

A lower-friction alternative is a free standalone experience with a EUR 25.99
non-consumable lifetime unlock. That lets users experience capture and recall
before paying, but introduces StoreKit implementation and support work. Apple
documents non-consumables as one-time purchases that do not expire in its
[in-app purchase overview](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/configure-in-app-purchase-settings/overview-for-configuring-in-app-purchases).

Start with paid upfront only if the app includes an excellent sample vault,
guided first-run demonstration, and short product video. Otherwise test the
lifetime-unlock model.

Do not call the purchase an "IP license." The customer receives a licence to
use the app; they do not acquire NeuroVault's intellectual property. Do not
advertise "originally EUR 39.99" unless real customers genuinely had an
opportunity to buy at that price. Configure genuine price changes through
[App Store Connect pricing](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/manage-app-pricing/set-a-price/).

## Store product boundary

Apple requires a Mac App Store app to be sandboxed, self-contained, based on
public APIs, and updated through the Store. See the [App Review Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/)
and [App Sandbox documentation](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/security/app-sandbox).

The Store app therefore cannot silently install hooks, write other apps'
configuration, own arbitrary paths, kill port-holding processes, run a custom
GitHub updater, or depend on a separately installed application for its basic
value. External folders require explicit selection and persistent
[security-scoped access](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/security/accessing-files-from-the-macos-app-sandbox).

The paid Store app must remain valuable by itself as a memory browser, editor,
search, graph, review/audit, import/export, and data-control application. The
MIT Core can optionally provide deeper host integration, but Store copy must
not imply that the sandboxed app silently installs it.

## Launch gate

Do not submit until a fresh user can complete all of these without developer
help:

1. Understand the promise within ten seconds of launch.
2. Get meaningful standalone value without Node, Core, or another AI app.
3. Create or import memory through a sandbox-compliant flow.
4. Connect at least Claude and Codex through a guided, permission-respecting
   path.
5. Witness capture, recall, and an evidence trail quickly.
6. See exactly where data lives and delete or export it.
7. Correct memory without being turned into a full-time reviewer.
8. Use the main consumer loop without opening Terminal.
9. Complete an offline first run after installation.
10. Pass signing, sandbox, privacy manifest, accessibility, and App Review
    checks documented in `docs/APP-STORE-READINESS.md`.

## Validation plan

The first objective is not 10,000 purchases. It is evidence from people who
are not friends and did not build the product.

1. Recruit 15 to 25 target users who use at least two AI clients and repeat
   project context regularly.
2. Observe five unassisted first runs before changing pricing.
3. Measure time to first stored memory, first useful recall, first correction,
   seven-day return, support minutes, and willingness to pay.
4. Test one clear product page around cross-AI continuity and evidence, not a
   long feature inventory.
5. Launch at EUR 25.99 only when at least several testers say they would be
   disappointed to lose it and can explain the value without using the word
   MCP.
6. Treat 100 paid strangers as the first commercial milestone.

The durable commercial structure is:

- public MIT Core for trust, adoption, integrations, and technical users;
- paid Desktop for polish, visualisation, setup, review, audit, data controls,
  lifecycle management, and support;
- the Mac App Store as one distribution channel, not the complete growth
  strategy.
